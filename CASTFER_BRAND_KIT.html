Voice & Tone

Direct

No corporate speak. No "we're committed to providing." Say what you mean. Short sentences. Strong verbs. The client has 3 seconds.

Bilingual

Spanish is not a translation — it's a first language. Write Spanish copy natively, not word-for-word from English. Never mix languages in a single sentence.

No BS

Don't use fake stats. Don't say "thousands of contractors." Don't claim awards that don't exist. Trust is earned through honesty, especially in the Hispanic market.

For the Working Class

Speak to someone who works with their hands. Respect their time. Acknowledge the reality of their job — GCs demanding last-minute COIs, surprise audits, getting underbid.

Urgent but Not Pushy

Create urgency around real problems (losing a job, an active job site). Never manufacture fake deadlines or use countdown timers that reset.

Human

There's a real person behind Castfer. William speaks Spanish natively, is local, and picks up the phone. That's the differentiation — lean into it.

Write like this

  • "Need insurance today? Call us now."
  • "We get you covered before the job starts."
  • "Lost a bid over a missing COI? Never again."
  • "¿Te pidieron seguro hoy? Nosotros te resolvemos."

Not like this

  • "We are committed to providing insurance solutions."
  • "Thousands of satisfied contractors trust Castfer."
  • "45% of contractors face bid rejections."
  • "Our industry-leading team is here to help you."
